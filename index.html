MY PHILOSOPHY

I'm a half self taught and half formally educated portrait and lifestyle photographer. I believe that every space has a story to tell, and my job is to create timeless images that evoke joy, warmth, and nostalgia, preserving precious memories for generations to come. The Nature of Reality: Philosophers like Plato and Immanuel Kant explored the relationship between the world we experience through our senses and the "true" reality. As a photographer, you might ponder how your images reflect or distort reality. Do they capture the essence of the moment, or do they create a new, subjective reality? Perception and Perspective: Philosophers like Ren√© Descartes and Friedrich Nietzsche wrote about the role of perception in shaping our understanding of the world. As a photographer, you're aware of how different lenses, angles, and lighting can alter the viewer's perception of the subject. This parallels the philosophical idea that our individual perspectives influence our understanding of reality. Aesthetics and Beauty: Philosophers like Aristotle and Kant discussed the nature of beauty and taste. As a photographer, you likely have a keen eye for composition, lighting, and other elements that contribute to a visually appealing image. Philosophical discussions around aesthetics can help you better understand the ways in which beauty is perceived and experienced. Time and Memory: Philosophers like Henri Bergson and Martin Heidegger explored the human experience of time and memory. As a photographer, you often capture moments in time, preserving memories for yourself and others. Philosophical reflections on time and memory can deepen your understanding of the significance of these captured moments.
